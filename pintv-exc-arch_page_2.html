05:33:09

So worldlines basically act as a stand in for the tracing pattern of a 3rd dimensional being's fourth dimension, only this fourth dimension isn't another dimension of space like a cube is to a square, but time. So a person's world line would look like if you took a big marker, drew a huge line that followed each place the person was in the universe across their entire life from birth to death, and stepped back to look at it like a piece of abstract art. That big squiggle you just drew out? World line baby. Thats the 4th dimension we can't see, because instead of being able to see the big squiggle from where you are in the world (aka where you are on earth at each moment of your entire lifetime), you can only see the 3 dimensions you exist in: the height, breadth and depth of the world around you. Sure you can perceive time and how the social construct of it works, but you can't see your world line from start to finish – only the but that you're currently existing in, like how a 2 dimensional creature can only see part of a 3 dimensional object. If you draw a square and trace out the dashes that would connect to it to make a cube, you have your existing dimension (the opaque square lines) and your world lines (the dashes that form the cube). So if a 2 dimensional creature was given the ability to view its world lines, it would be able to see those traced dashes that intersect across its square while still only really existing in the second dimension. If you gave a 3 dimensional being the abiltity to percieve its world line. it would be experiencing everything that happens, where it happens in its life all simulatneously. Okay, so the compact cassette was invented around 1964 and the brand Compact Cassette was put in place in 1965. The hight of the popularity of CCs was in the 80s, and by the 90s they were much much less used. However up into the 90s they were still being used in certain ways, one of which being recording things like interviews, or notes, or personal dictation. I could not figure out exactly what kind of Compact Cassette it was, but the C-60 indicates that it is a double sided tape and that there are 30 minutes of recording time on either side. The C-60 tapes were the most common and the most widely used. With all of this, it is unlikely that we could date the tape to a specific time.

"Is it...dangerous?" Oliver's voice wavered slightly as he looked at Kogan. Kogan hesitated, searching for the right words, "Not to you... I don't think." Oliver nodded thoughtfully. Kogan wished he could offer more assurance, but even he didn't properly know the answer. There was a part of him, a selfish part, that hoped Oliver would give him the benefit of the doubt and stay by his side. Oliver's next question was gentler still, "Are you afraid?" As Kogan met Oliver's gaze, a whirlwind of emotions surged within him. The familiar defensive instinct urged him to rebuff Oliver's pity. But there was a competing urge, one that had Kogan marveling at the unwavering strength behind his friend's concerned eyes, and made him long to accept the promise of protection and solace that lay there, if only he could somehow crawl inside and—Kogan abruptly halted that unsettling train of thought and averted his eyes. Kogan exhaled shakily and the ache in his chest deepened as he reminded himself that he had already become the horror that Oliver thought he could protect him from. "Yes, Oliver." Kogan whispered, "I'm terrified." As silence stretched between them, Kogan thought about asking the same question; was Oliver afraid? But the words choked in his throat for fear of the answer. "Can I see it?" Oliver's asked. Kogan blinked. He wasn't sure why the request surprised him. It was logical to want to evaluate a threat with your own eyes, after all. Still, he was hesitant. The horrified expressions of the last people to have seen... 'it'... floated in his mind's eye. Kogan swallowed thickly. He wasn't sure if he was ready to see that look on Oliver's face. But Oliver deserved to know, he told himself. Oliver sat quietly in front of him, giving him the time he needed. After a moment, Kogan nodded and slowly opened his mouth. Oliver did an admirable job of keeping his expression neutral. If not for Kogan's anxiety-induced hyperawareness, he probably wouldn't have noticed the deeper intake of breath or the slight widening of Oliver's eyes as something more like an insect larva than a human tongue emerged from Kogan's lips. The semi-translucent appendage undulated and glowed under the dim light. The surface seemed to shift and pull tight as several peculiar pairs of nubby legs knitted together like a caterpillar cradling itself against a cool breeze.
